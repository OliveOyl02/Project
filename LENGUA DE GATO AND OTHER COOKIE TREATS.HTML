LENGUA DE GATO AND OTHER COOKIE TREATS

        Lengua de Gato literally means “cat’s tongue” in Spanish, and since these cookies are shaped like a cat’s tongue, they are named so. They are simple yet delicious butter cookies which are always available and in abundance at Baguio’s public markets. There are some brands that are more famous than others, but the important part is that you choose the jars with the least crushed cookies in them. Lengua de Gato, as good as they taste, are very brittle, unless you prefer them smaller.
        If you prefer cookies that give you a fuller bite, try the chocolate crinkles, or better yet, some Alfajor cookies, which are lemon butter cookies with caramel filling. Crinkles are widely available at the public market, but only Good Shepherd makes Alfajors.
