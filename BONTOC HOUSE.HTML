Bontoc Tribe or Bontoks The Bontoks, according to the Book of the Philippines, 1976, are “the fiercest and most warlike but their advanced ways make them the most sociable of all the tribes.” They are the native inhabitants of Bontoc, Mountain Province.
        The Bontoc have a tradition of cloth weaving. The background colors are dark, the favorite being blue. Geometric designs are diamonds, triangles, hexagons and zigzags. Representational designs are the dancing man or woman, stars, leaves, and rice paddies. These are woven in yellow, green, white and red threads. These designs are used in garments and blankets.
        The pre-Christian Bontoc belief system centers on a hierarchy of spirits, the highest being a supreme deity called Lumawig.
         Although the Bontoc believe in the anito or spirits of their ancestors and in spirits dwelling in nature, they are essentially monotheistic . The Bontoc also believe in the "anito"—spirits of the dead who must be consulted before anything important is done.
